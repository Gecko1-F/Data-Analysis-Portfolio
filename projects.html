01

Liqueur Sales MX

📊 Tableau Completed: June 2026

🎯 Business Case

A multi-regional retail enterprise faced declining visibility into localized sales performance, leaving leadership unable to identify underperforming product segments in real-time. This project establishes an interactive business intelligence framework designed to eliminate data blind spots, optimize inventory categories, and scale dynamically to support automated retail data ingestion pipelines.

📊 Dataset

  • Source: Liqueur Sales MX
  • Size: 5,718 transactions
  • Period: 04/2022
  • Fields: 33 columns including Order Date, Customer ID, City, Country, Region, Product, SKU, Price, Cost, Revenue

🔧 Approach & Process

  1. Data Cleaning: Removed 47 duplicate records and standardized date formats using SQL
  2. Data Modeling: Created calculated fields for gross margin & net revenue
  3. Dashboard Design: Built interactive dashboard with filters for region, category, and product sales to enable deep-dive root cause analysis.
  4. Insights Generation: Dug into the regional data to find out exactly why certain areas were losing money, allowing me to pinpoint underperforming locations and suggest areas where to focus.

💡 Key Findings

  • Regional Performance: A significant portion of total revenue (82.92%) is concentrated within three key regions. Region 3 is the largest contributor at 35.39%, followed closely by Region 4 and Region 5, which account for 24.23% and 23.30% respectively
  • Channel Sales distribution is heavily concentrated in the Traditional channel at 90.45%, with Modeloramas representing the second-largest segment at 7.54%
  • Product All four products demonstrated comparable performance. Peach liqueur generated the highest sales share at 27.84%, with strawberry liqueur following at 25.50%. Mango and pineapple contributed nearly equal shares at 23.49% and 23.17%, respectively.
  • Underperformance: Regions 1, 2, and 6 deliver marginal results, collectively accounting for just 14.11% of total sales. Region 1 registers the lowest performance with a nominal 2.04% market share. A strategic review is recommended to determine if these underperforming figures are cyclical, constrained by limited marketing spend, or driven by regional consumer preferences and cultural habits.

🛠️ Tools Used

Tableau Desktop SQL Excel Data Cleaning

02

Obesity Levels

📈 Power BI Completed: November 2025

🎯 Business Case

This dashboard models and estimates individual obesity risk across Mexico, Peru, and Colombia. By correlating behavioral dietary habits with physical fitness metrics, the project provides data-driven insights into regional health profiles, enabling healthcare stakeholders to identify high-risk lifestyle cohorts and optimize targeted wellness interventions.

📊 Dataset

  • Source: Obesity Levels Dataset via Kaggle (Uploaded by F. Mehrparvar)
  • Size: 2,111 unique records
  • Fields: 17 attributes including gender, weight, age, high calorie intake, etc.
  • Data Lineage Note Composed of 23% empirical survey data and 77% synthetically generated records (via SMOTE) to resolve regional class imbalances.

🔧 Approach & Process

  1. Cleaned and transformed data using Power Query
  2. Built robust DAX measures to dynamically calculate Body Mass Index (BMI), conditional Weight Status tiers, and custom Age Group cohorts..
  3. Created a multi-page report with executive summary, lifestyle impact, and demographic analysis

💡 Key Findings

  • High Prevalence of Obesity: 46.0% of all study participants are classified as obese, exhibiting a Body Mass Index (BMI) greater than 30.
  • Demographic Activity Trends: The Young Adult (20–29) cohort represents the most physically active segment, with 59.36% engagement.
  • Gender-Specific Severity: Clinical severity varies significantly by gender; 31.0% of female participants present with Class III (Severe) Obesity (BMI > 40), while 27.62% of male participants present with Class II Obesity (BMI between 35.0 and 39.9).

🛠️ Tools Used

Power BI DAX Power Query

    Lamborghini Sales 2020 - 2025

    📉 Looker Studio Completed: April 2026

    🎯 Business Case

    Lamborghini's demand patterns (order volumes, wait times, pricing power) serve as a leading indicator of ultra-high-net-worth (UHNW) consumer confidence and capital deployment behavior. A recession typically manifests in UHNW spending hesitation 6–12 months before official macro indicators (yield curve inversion, unemployment data, GDP contraction) confirm economic slowdown. The organization needs to develop a predictive framework that uses Lamborghini demand elasticity data to identify recession signals early enough to execute defensive portfolio repositioning.

    📊 Dataset

    • Source: Lamborghini Global Sales Dataset (2020–2025) via Kaggle (Curated by Z. Nusratt)
    • Size: 54 unique records
    • Fields: 10 attributes including model, region, Base Price (USD), Sales Volume, etc.

    💡 Key Findings

    • Product Performance: Urus (Gasoline) established itself as the primary portfolio growth driver, generating a dominant $13.0B in cumulative revenue from 2020 through 2025.
    • Consumer Preference: Black remains the highly preferred aesthetic choice globally, accounting for 31.76% of all model configurations sold.
    • Geographic Distribution: The Americas continues to be the brand's cornerstone market, capturing 39.37% of total global sales volume.
    • Recession Signaling: Velocity and volume metrics remained resilient through Q4 2025 in core UHNW segments, indicating that consumer spending in the luxury automotive layer has not yet crossed the threshold of a defensive defensive macro pullback.
